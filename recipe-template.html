Fettuccine with Shiitake Mushrooms & Basil

A healthy whole wheat pasta dish that only takes 10 minutes of prep time.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
  • 3/4 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/2 ounce shiitake mushrooms, stemmed and sliced
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly grated lemon zest
  • 1/2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • pinch of salt, to taste
  • Freshly ground pepper, to taste
  • 2 ounces whole-wheat fettucine
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh basil
  • 2 tablespoons freshly grated Parmesan cheese

Process

  1. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil for cooking pasta
  2. Heat oil in large nonstick skillet over low heat. Add garlic and cook,
    stirring until fragrant but not browned, about 1 minute. Add mushrooms
    and increase heat to medium-high; cook, stirring occassionally, until tender
    and lightly browned, 4 to 5 minutes. Stir in lemon zest, lemon juice,
    salt and pepper. Remove from the heat.
  3. Meanwhile, cook pasta, stirring occasionally, until tender, 9 to 11 minutes.
    Drain, reserving 2 tablespoons cooking liquid.
  4. Add the pasta, the reserved cooking liquid, Parmesan, and 1/4 cup basil to the
    mushrooms in the skillet; toss to coat well. Serve immediately, garnished
    with remaining basil.

Southwestern Cheese Panini

A cheesy sandwich full of lots of vegetablese and salsa that's perfect for lunch.

Ingredients

  • 1 ounce shredded sharp Cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup shredded zucchini
  • 1/8 cup shredded carrot
  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped red onion
  • 1 tablespoon prepared salsa
  • 1/4 tablespoon chopped pickled jalapeno pepper
  • 2 slices whole-wheat bread
  • 1/2 teaspoons canola oil

Process

  1. Combine Cheddar, zucchini, carrot, onion, salsa, and jalapeno in a medium bowl.
    Spread onto slice of bread and top with the remaining slice of bread.
  2. Heat canola oil in a large nonstick skillet over medium heat. Place the panini in
    the pan. Place a medium skillet on top of the panini to weigh it down. Cook
    the panini until golden on one side, about 2 minutes. Reduce the heat to medium-
    low, flip the panini, replace the top skillet, and cook until the second side
    is golden, 1 to 3 more minutes.

Moroccan Chicken Salad

A flavorful main dish salad featuring chicken and rice.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup cooked rice
  • 1/2 cup shredded cooked chicken
  • 1/2 cup shredded carrots
  • 1 tablespoon sliced scallions
  • 1 tablespoon chopped Kalamata olives
  • 1 tablespoon reduced-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon orange juice
  • 1/2 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 tablespoon chopped fresh mint
  • 1/2 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
  • Pinch of ground cinnamon
  • Pinch of cayenne pepper
  • Salt and freshly ground pepper, to taste

Process

  1. Combine rice, chicken, carrots, scallions, and olives in a large bowl.
  2. Whisk together orange juice, lemon juice, mint, cilantro, oil, cinnamon, cayenne,
    and salt and pepper in another bowl to form dressing.
  3. Toss rice mixture with dressing.

Szechuan Tofu and Green Bean Stir-Fry

A spicy vegetarian stir-fry.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons water
  • 1 tablespoon reduced-sodium soy sauce
  • 1/4 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon Chinkiang vinegar or balsamic vinegar
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • Pinch of crushed red pepper
  • 1/2 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 4 ounces extra-firm tofu, drained
  • 1 cup green beans, trimmed and cut in half
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon minced fresh ginger

Process

  1. Whisk water, soy sauce, tomato paste, vinegar, sugar, crushed red pepper, and
    cornstarch in a small bowl. Cut tofu into 1/2 inch cubes and pat dry. Toss
    tofu in a bowl with cornstarch to coat.
  2. Heat oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the tofu and spread
    out across the surface of the pan. Let cook for 2 minutes. Gently turn and
    stir. Continue cooking, stirring occassionally, until lightly browned and
    crispy. Transfer to a plate.
  3. Reduce heat to medium. Add the remaining oil to the pan. Add green beans, garlic,
    and ginger. Cook, stirring constantly, for 1 minute. Add the remaining water,
    cover and cook until the beans are crisp-tender. Stir the reserved soy sauce
    mixture and pour it over the green beans. cook, stirring until thickened. Add
    the tofu and cook, stirring until headed through, about 1 minute more.
